Here are some key and important effects of floods:

  • 1. Emergency Supplies Kit:- Stock at least three days of bottled water, nonperishable food, flashlights, batteries, a first-aid kit, and essential medications.
  • 2.Home Safeguarding: Reinforce roofs with hurricane straps or clips; secure loose roofing materials.
  • 3.Evacuation and Communication Plan: Identify official evacuation routes and designate an inland shelter location..

  • 1.Shelter Protocols: Move to an interior room or purpose-built cyclone shelter away from windows.
  • 2.Information and Alerts: Monitor official sources (meteorological department bulletins, emergency services) via radio or mobile alerts.
  • 3.Electrical and Fuel Safety: Turn off main power, gas, and water supplies if instructed or if flooding is imminent.

  • 1.Wind-Resistant Building Codes: Enforce and update codes requiring reinforced concrete or steel framing designed for high wind loads.
  • 2.Elevated Foundations and Floodproofing: Elevate structures above projected storm-surge levels; use flood-resistant materials for lower walls.
  • 3.Site Selection and Drainage Planning: Avoid building on low-lying, flood-prone land or near erodible shorelines; install proper grading and retention basins.

  • 1.Roof and Shutter Reinforcements:Retrofit roof connections with metal straps; install RNase-rated storm shutters over all openings.
  • 2. Impact-Resistant Openings: Replace standard windows and doors with tested impact-resistant units or add reinforced boarding systems..
  • 3.Stormwater Management Enhancements: Add French drains, sump pumps, and rain barrels to divert runoff; reinforce drainage channels around the property.

  • 1.First Aid and Safety Checks: Check yourself and household members for injuries; administer first aid and call for professional help if needed.
  • 2. Utility Inspection and Shut-off: Inspect gas lines, electrical panels, and water pipes for damage; shut off compromised utilities to prevent fires and flooding.
  • 3.Damage Documentation and Insurance: Photograph structural damage, interior losses, and property debris; compile records for insurance claims.
  • 4.Relief and Recovery Coordination: Contact local relief agencies for emergency shelter, clean water, and medical aid.
